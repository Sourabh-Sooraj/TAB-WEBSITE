The Live Ball of Fire by TM Madhumitha
Toastmasters Amrita Bengaluru is one of the most dynamic clubs of the college. I took up my position as President of the club right when the entire world went into a lockdown. It was a transitioning period from offline meetings to completely online meetings. Major challenge was to warm up the club members to circumstances which are poles apart. Nevertheless the club has always managed to stir in the right direction with the help of the right mentors. The term was extremely successful in terms of motivation of the members, membership count, DCP and the awards. I owe most of it to my team which had put in a lot of hardwork and effort in bringing the club to a point of self sustenance.

A college club is in fact very different from a corporate and community club. One major factor being the members. The members of a college club are college going students who are incredibly energetic and enthusiastic. To give a more fancy analogy it is like dealing with a live ball of fire. We will have to guide them to channel the energy into the right direction, just so that they get the best out of everything. Being a leader in a club which has both seniors and juniors was a healthy challenge. The club environment was made very positive and extremely nurturing to the members. This also helped in establishing a robust relationship between the members and between the leadership team.

One of the perks of being a college club is, everyone is in the same age bracket. Thus the thinking, perspectives and way of handling things are more or less similar. This aided a lot in the smooth functioning of the club and propelled us to get the highest possible awards.

Toastmasters Amrita Bengaluru is one of the most dynamic clubs of the college and will probably remain the same till the end.
